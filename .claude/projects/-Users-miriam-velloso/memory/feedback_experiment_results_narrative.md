Experiment results text on review slides should be narrative bullet points — each bullet is 1-2 sentences explaining a finding with context, separated by paragraph breaks for readability.

**Why:** Raw metric bullets ("+0.46% (not sig)") are hard to read for non-data audiences. Dense paragraphs are also bad — they don't scan. Narrative bullets with bold highlights are the sweet spot.

**How to apply:**
- Each bullet starts with ● and explains one finding in plain language ("driven by", "likely because", "suggesting")
- Bold metric names and key percentages so they pop visually
- No statistical jargon (no "p-value", "confidence interval", "MDE") — say "conclusive", "reliable", "within noise"
- For non-significant primary metrics, say "flat", "directionally positive/negative", or "trending up/down"
- Separate bullets with paragraph breaks (\n\n) for visual breathing room
- Poppins 7pt font for the results text box
- 4-5 bullets per slide is ideal
